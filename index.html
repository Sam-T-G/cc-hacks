01 New here? Start with this

What's a hackathon?

It's an event where students team up and build something real: an app, a website, a small device, anything that goes from a blank screen to a working demo. Picture a science fair, but for building things, with mentors in the room the whole time and food on the table. At the end, every team shows what they made, and judges hand out prizes.

Questions coordinators usually ask

Do students need to know how to code?
No, and that is the point. Beginners are exactly who we want. Workshops and mentors get everyone started on day one, and many projects lean on design, research, and presentation as much as code.
Is this only for computer science majors?
No. It is open to any community college student. STEM students thrive here, and the strongest teams mix coders with designers, planners, and presenters.
Is it stressful or cutthroat?
It is collaborative by design. Most students come to learn and build something with friends. Winning a prize is a bonus on top of that.
What do they actually make?
Working things they can show off: apps, websites, simple hardware, data projects. The kind of project that goes straight onto a resume or a transfer application.

02 Two ways to run it

One focused day, or a full 24 with MLH.

Both are real plans, not a default and a maybe. We can run a strong single day ourselves, or go a full 24 hours with MLH, the official student hackathon league, as a partner.

Self-run / about 9 hours

One day

A complete hackathon between breakfast and dinner. Lower lift, lower cost, and a strong first event we run end to end.

  1. 9:00 AMKickoffDoors open, breakfast, and the theme drops.
  2. 9:30 AMTeam upTeams of up to four. Came alone? We help you find a group.
  3. 10:00 AMBuildMentors and quick workshops on tap all day.
  4. 4:30 PMDemoTeams show their project at a science-fair-style expo.
  5. 6:00 PMAwardsJudges pick favorites. Everyone leaves with a project.

Best for a strong, low-risk first year.

/ 24 hours, overnight

24 hours

A full overnight build, backed by MLH's platform, mentor network, swag, and national credibility. The bigger stage.

  1. Sat 10:00 AMOpening ceremonyMLH welcome, theme reveal, and team formation.
  2. Sat 11:00 AMBuild beginsMentors roam; MLH and sponsor workshops run all afternoon.
  3. Sat 7:00 PMDinner + mini-eventsFood, games, and a breather with the whole room.
  4. OvernightHack through the nightA quiet space, snacks, and momentum. Build while it's fresh.
  5. Sun 10:00 AMSubmit + demoProjects go in, and teams demo at the expo.
  6. Sun 12:30 PMClosing + awardsJudging wraps and prizes go out, MLH pins included.

Best for maximum scale, resources, and reach.

03 Why MESA

exists for these students.
So does CC Hacks.

MESA supports educationally and financially disadvantaged students to excel in STEM and transfer. A community-college-only hackathon puts those same students in front of a project, a mentor, and a network for a day. The audiences are not similar. They are the same.

For your students, that means hands-on experience, a portfolio piece, and exposure to the Google developer community. Each one strengthens a transfer application.

  • For studentsA real project, mentors, and a network in one day.
  • For MESAA high-visibility STEM event, organized for you, not by you.
  • For the regionCommunity college talent, finally on a stage built for it.

05 Your involvement / you choose

Pick the level that fits.

RCC MESA and MVC MESA can choose different levels. Tap a card to try one on. Nothing here is all-or-nothing.

We would be glad to have MESA at any level.

An optional MESA exclusive

Run your own MESA track.

Either branch can sponsor a private prize track for its own students. Any team where the majority of members are in your MESA program competes for a cash prize your branch puts up. It is a built-in reason for your students to show up, and a spotlight that is yours alone. RCC MESA and MVC MESA can each run their own.

Who qualifies
Teams that are majority MESA students
The prize
Cash, in an amount your branch sets
The credit
Your name on the track, your students in the running
